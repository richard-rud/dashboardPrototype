Is food waste an increasing problem?

Food waste seems to be an increasing problem. About one third of all the food that is being produced is wasted. The developing world is leading the way, each year throwing away almost the same amount of food as is being produced in all of sub-Sharan Africa, nearly 230 million ton/yr

What are the current strategies?

Traditionaly as seen from the pyramid, the EU focus has been on using the waste hierarchy principles, where prevention of food waste is top priority. In Norway strategies however tend to favor recycling solutions (grey).

Why do we need systems-wide understanding?

We do not blindly want to follow the waste hierarchy, since it lacks information about, mass, energy and critical material flows for food production. Since we are dealing with a multi-dimensional problem, we have to consider system-wide effects, since food waste affects multiple actors as well as environmental resources (Hamilton 2015). For this job, we use MFA (material flow anlysis), we quantifies the flows and stocks of a certain system (source: x), in this case the Norwegian food waste system.
